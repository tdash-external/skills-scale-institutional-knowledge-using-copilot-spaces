# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync

## Post-Mortem Checklist (for Incidents & Significant Failures)

Use this checklist after any production incident, missed milestone, or significant quality failure.

**Preparation (within 24 hours of incident):**
- [ ] Incident timeline documented (what happened, when, who was involved)
- [ ] All affected stakeholders notified
- [ ] Immediate remediation actions recorded

**Post-Mortem Meeting (within 3 business days):**
- [ ] Meeting scheduled with all involved parties
- [ ] Blameless framing agreed — focus on systems, not individuals
- [ ] Timeline reviewed and verified by participants
- [ ] Root cause(s) identified (use "5 Whys" or similar technique)
- [ ] Contributing factors documented (process gaps, tooling issues, communication breakdowns)
- [ ] Impact assessed (users affected, duration, severity, business impact)
- [ ] Short-term mitigations already applied confirmed effective

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Preventive actions identified with owners and due dates
- [ ] Process or documentation updates identified
- [ ] Monitoring or alerting improvements identified
- [ ] Action items added to project backlog

**Follow-Up:**
- [ ] Post-mortem notes published and shared with relevant stakeholders
- [ ] Action items reviewed in next weekly PM sync
- [ ] Learnings added to team knowledge base or retrospective history
